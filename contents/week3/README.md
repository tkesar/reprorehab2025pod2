# Week 3: Tabular data with Pandas II (Intermediate)

## Learning objective of the week
You will practice creating, reshaping, and filtering tabular data using core pandas functions such as `.concat()`, `.melt()`, and `.pivot()`.

## Why?
**(a) Recognizing universal data-manipulation patterns**

This week's material trains learners to recognize *common data-manipulation motifs* that appear in virtually *every* research or analytics project:
- "Combine small files into one large dataset."
- "Reshape long <-> wide for stats or visualization."
- "Filter columns dynamically by name patterns."

These operations may seem routine, but they're foundational.
  
**(b) Developing research independence**

You'll also see how much of the tedious data wrangling can be automated in Python, so that you don't always have to worry about inconsistent formats, missing columns, or other quirks in raw datasets - many of which are beyond your control.
